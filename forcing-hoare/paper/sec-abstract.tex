\section{An Abstract Forcing Framework}
\label{sec:abstract}

The proofs of Sections~\ref{sec:prop}--\ref{sec:lam} used
$(\omega, \le)$ throughout, but none required $\omega$ specifically.
We abstract the framework over an arbitrary well-founded forcing
notion and prove Löb's rule once at this level of generality.  Two
claims become precise.  First, the choice of $\omega$ is a
presentation convenience, not a foundational commitment.  Second, the
relationship between Löb's rule and well-founded induction becomes a
literal identity: the abstract \texttt{iLob} is proved by
\texttt{apply (well\_founded\_ind fs\_lt\_wf)}.

\paragraph{The forcing structure.}
\begin{lstlisting}
Record ForcingStructure : Type := MkFS {
  fs_cond : Type;
  fs_le : fs_cond -> fs_cond -> Prop;
  fs_lt : fs_cond -> fs_cond -> Prop;
  fs_le_refl : forall p, fs_le p p;
  fs_le_trans : forall p q r,
                  fs_le p q -> fs_le q r -> fs_le p r;
  fs_lt_le : forall p q, fs_lt p q -> fs_le p q;
  fs_lt_le_lt : forall p q r,
                  fs_lt p q -> fs_le q r -> fs_lt p r;
  fs_lt_wf : well_founded fs_lt }.
\end{lstlisting}
\noindent
A forcing structure is a preorder
$(\mathtt{fs\_cond}, \mathtt{fs\_le})$ with a strict relation
$\mathtt{fs\_lt}$ contained in $\mathtt{fs\_le}$ and right-compatible
with it, such that $\mathtt{fs\_lt}$ is well-founded.  The
compatibility axiom $\mathtt{fs\_lt\_le\_lt}$ is what makes the later
modality well-defined.

\paragraph{iProp and the later modality, abstractly.}
Over an arbitrary $\mathtt{FS}$, \texttt{iProp} is a downward-closed
predicate on $\mathtt{fs\_cond}\, \mathtt{FS}$. The Heyting
connectives are defined exactly as in Section~\ref{sec:prop} with
$\le$ replaced by $\mathtt{fs\_le}\, \mathtt{FS}$.  The interesting
case is the later modality.  At $(\omega, \le)$ we defined
$\triangleright \varphi$ at depth $n$ by a match on $n$.  At an
arbitrary forcing notion we cannot match on conditions, so we give
$\triangleright$ a uniform definition: it holds at $p$ iff $\varphi$
holds at every strict predecessor of $p$:
\begin{lstlisting}
Definition iLater (φ : iProp) : iProp :=
  MkProp (fun p => forall p', fs_lt FS p' p -> p' ⊩ φ)
         (iLater_mono_helper φ).
\end{lstlisting}
\noindent
At a minimal condition this is vacuously \texttt{True}.  At
$(\omega, \le)$ the new definition agrees with the old one by
downward closure: ``$\varphi$ holds at every $m < n+1$'' is, for
downward-closed $\varphi$, the same as ``$\varphi$ holds at $n$.''

\paragraph{Löb's rule, by well-founded induction.}
\begin{lstlisting}
Lemma iLob (φ : iProp) : (▷ φ → φ) ⊢ φ.
Proof.
  intros p. revert p.
  apply (well_founded_ind (fs_lt_wf FS)
                          (fun p => p ⊩ (▷ φ → φ) -> p ⊩ φ)).
  intros p IH Hp.
  apply Hp; [apply (fs_le_refl FS)|].
  intros p' Hp'.
  apply IH; [exact Hp'|].
  eapply imono; [apply (fs_lt_le FS); exact Hp' | exact Hp].
Qed.
\end{lstlisting}
\noindent
Well-founded induction on $p$: at each $p$, the induction hypothesis
gives $\varphi$ at every strict predecessor $p'$.  Apply $\mathtt{Hp}$
at $p$ itself (using $\mathtt{fs\_le\_refl}$ to witness $p \le p$),
leaving $\triangleright \varphi$ at $p$ to prove, which by the
definition of $\triangleright$ is the quantified statement that
$\varphi$ holds at every strict predecessor, available from the IH.
This is the literal statement of the equivalence: \emph{Löb's rule is
well-founded induction on the forcing order, internalized into the
logic}.

\paragraph{Instances.}
The natural numbers with their usual order:
\begin{lstlisting}
Definition nat_FS : ForcingStructure :=
  {| fs_cond := nat;
     fs_le := Nat.le;  fs_lt := Nat.lt;
     fs_le_refl := Nat.le_refl;  fs_le_trans := Nat.le_trans;
     fs_lt_le := Nat.lt_le_incl;
     fs_lt_le_lt := Nat.lt_le_trans;  fs_lt_wf := lt_wf |}.
\end{lstlisting}
\noindent
At $\mathtt{nat\_FS}$, the abstract framework recovers
Section~\ref{sec:prop}: $\mathtt{Check}\, \mathtt{iLob}\, \mathtt{nat\_FS}$
confirms that the abstract \texttt{iLob} specialized to the
natural-number instance is the entailment we expect.

A non-trivially-different second instance is the pointwise product
$\omega \times \omega$.  Define
$\mathtt{le\_pair}\, p\, q := \mathtt{fst}\, p \le \mathtt{fst}\, q \wedge \mathtt{snd}\, p \le \mathtt{snd}\, q$
and $\mathtt{lt\_pair}\, p\, q := \mathtt{le\_pair}\, p\, q \wedge p \neq q$.
This poset is not totally ordered: $(1,0)$ and $(0,1)$ are
incomparable.  Well-foundedness of $\mathtt{lt\_pair}$ follows from
the measure $\mathtt{fst}\, p + \mathtt{snd}\, p$ into $\mathbb{N}$
via the standard library lemma $\mathtt{well\_founded\_lt\_compat}$,
giving $\mathtt{prod\_FS} : \mathtt{ForcingStructure}$.  At this
instance the framework provides a step-indexed logic with two
independent step counters: a natural model for two parallel
components, where Löb's rule lets one reason about recursion that
descends in either or both components.

A further family we do not develop but which deserves mention is the
\emph{transfinite} one.  Countable ordinals up to a bound form
well-founded preorders, and so does the index structure used in
Transfinite Iris~\cite{spies2021transfinite}.  At any such instance,
the framework's \texttt{iLob} is proved by exactly the same
\texttt{well\_founded\_ind} on $\mathtt{fs\_lt}$.  The transfinite
choice produces a stronger step-indexed logic without changing the
soundness machinery.  The framework subsumes the index structure of
Transfinite Iris in the same sense it subsumes the
$(\omega, \le)$ structure of ordinary Iris, as a particular
instantiation of a generic well-founded-forcing framework.

\paragraph{Soundness of propositional GL.}
As a concrete check, we define syntactic \textsf{GL} formulas
\texttt{gl\_form}, interpret them into $\mathtt{iProp}$ with
$\Box \mapsto \triangleright$, and verify the modal core: axioms K,
4, L\"ob, and the necessitation rule (file \texttt{Phase5b\_GL.v}).
Each axiom unfolds to a small step-index calculation, with L\"ob's
case a direct invocation of $\mathtt{iLob}$.  This confirms concretely
what the topos-of-trees literature establishes abstractly: our
$\mathtt{iProp}$-with-$\triangleright$ structure is a sound model of
the modal core of \textsf{GL}.

We have shown that the data of a step-indexed logic is captured by a
\texttt{ForcingStructure} record, that the propositional fragment is
parametric in this data, that the internal Löb rule is, in any such
structure, a direct instance of well-founded induction, and that the
standard $(\omega, \le)$ choice is one instance, with at least one
non-trivially-different alternative.  The IMP and $\lambda$-calculus
developments of Sections~\ref{sec:imp}--\ref{sec:lam} can be lifted
to be parametric in the forcing structure with mostly bookkeeping
changes, not done here for readability.
