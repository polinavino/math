\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have given a forcing-style semantics for step-indexed Hoare logic,
mechanized in Rocq, and used it to articulate two technical
observations.

The first is the recursion-locus diagnostic of
Section~\ref{sec:imp-vs-lam}.  In the first-order setting of IMP, the
\texttt{while} rule's soundness predicate is recursive only in the
step index, and the two natural soundness proofs --- outer induction
on $n$ and an appeal to the internal Löb rule --- are structurally
identical.  Mechanizing both side by side makes the equivalence
concrete rather than analogical.  In the higher-order setting of
the $\lambda$-calculus with \texttt{fix}, the soundness predicate is
recursive in the program itself; meta-level induction on $n$ remains
available but no longer plays to the predicate's structure, while
the internal Löb rule does.  The structural difference is a
difference in the \emph{recursion locus}.

The second is the negative result of
Section~\ref{sec:lob-essential}.  We exhibit a would-be forcing
structure over $\mathbb{Z}$ in which every iProp construction goes
through but Löb's rule fails on $\bot$.  This is the step-indexed
Hoare logic counterpart of a classical fact about Gödel--Löb modal
logic; the statement does not, to our knowledge, appear in the
verification literature.  The methodological upshot is that the
choice of step-index domain is the choice that decides whether
Löb's rule is available; ordinary, transfinite, and product step
indices all pass the check, but the well-foundedness has to be
verified rather than assumed.

The abstract framework of Section~\ref{sec:abstract} makes precise
that the choice of $\omega$ as the step-index domain is a
computational convenience rather than a foundational one: any
well-founded forcing notion supports the same internal logic, with
$\triangleright$ defined as ``forces at every strict predecessor''
and Löb derived by \texttt{well\_founded\_ind}.
Section~\ref{sec:bridge} formalizes the embedding of meta-level
$\mathsf{Prop}$ into the propositional fragment of the JTS forcing
translation as a Heyting algebra homomorphism, with $\triangleright$
as the step-indexed-specific content lying outside the embedded
image.

\paragraph{Limitations and future directions.}
We work at the level of plain Hoare triples with a partial-correctness
wp predicate, without separation logic, heap state, or concurrency.
Whether the forcing reading scales gracefully to Iris-style separation
logic, resource state, invariants, and concurrent step counters is an
open question this paper does not answer; our contribution is the
diagnostic and the negative result, not an Iris-scale framework.  Four
natural extensions suggest themselves: mechanizing the type-level half
of the JTS correspondence (Section~\ref{sec:bridge}) as a theorem
rather than a heuristic; exhibiting non-trivial verifications at
richer forcing structures including transfinite step indices
\cite{spies2021transfinite}; lifting the IMP and $\lambda$-calculus
developments of Sections~\ref{sec:imp}--\ref{sec:lam} to be parametric
in the forcing structure; and integrating with separation logic on a
heap.  Whether the recursion-locus diagnostic can be sharpened to a
syntactic check on iProp definitions, applicable automatically to
Iris-style soundness proofs, is the question we are most interested
in pursuing.

The broader point we hope the paper makes is that step-indexed
reasoning, viewed as a forcing construction, sits at the intersection
of three research traditions that do not usually talk to each other:
set-theoretic forcing, modal logic of provability, and step-indexed
semantics for verification.  Each contains piece-of-the-picture
insights that the others could profit from; the mechanization
accompanying this paper is intended as evidence for the technical
claims and as a starting point for others wishing to explore the
intersection further.
