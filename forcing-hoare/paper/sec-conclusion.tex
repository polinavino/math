\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have given a forcing-style semantics for step-indexed Hoare logic,
mechanized in Rocq, and used it to articulate two technical
observations.

The first is the recursion-locus diagnostic of
Section~\ref{sec:imp-vs-lam}.  In the first-order setting of IMP, the
\texttt{while} rule's soundness predicate is recursive only in the
step index, and the two natural soundness proofs --- outer induction
on $n$ and an appeal to the internal Löb rule --- are structurally
identical.  Mechanizing both side by side makes the equivalence
concrete rather than analogical.  In the higher-order setting of
the $\lambda$-calculus with \texttt{fix}, the soundness predicate is
recursive in the program itself; meta-level induction on $n$ remains
available but no longer plays to the predicate's structure, while
the internal Löb rule does.  The structural difference is a
difference in the \emph{recursion locus}.

The second is the characterization of
Section~\ref{sec:lob-essential}.  In any forcing-style pre-structure
satisfying the order-theoretic axioms of the abstract framework,
L\"ob's rule holds for every iProp if and only if the strict
refinement relation is well-founded.  The backward direction
uses the forcing-accessibility predicate
$\varphi_{\mathrm{acc}}\, p := \mathtt{Acc}\, \mathtt{lt}\, p$ as an
internal iProp witness; one direction is realised concretely by a
would-be forcing structure over $\mathbb{Z}$ where every iProp
construction goes through but L\"ob's rule fails on $\bot$.  The iff
is a step-indexed Hoare logic counterpart of a classical fact about
G\"odel--L\"ob modal logic; the form mechanized here does not, to our
knowledge, appear in the verification literature.  The methodological
upshot is that the choice of step-index domain is the choice that
decides whether L\"ob's rule is available; ordinary, transfinite, and
product step indices all pass the check, but the well-foundedness
has to be verified rather than assumed.

The abstract framework of Section~\ref{sec:abstract} makes precise
that the choice of $\omega$ as the step-index domain is a
computational convenience rather than a foundational one: any
well-founded forcing notion supports the same internal logic, with
$\triangleright$ defined as ``forces at every strict predecessor''
and Löb derived by \texttt{well\_founded\_ind}.
Section~\ref{sec:bridge} formalizes the embedding of meta-level
$\mathsf{Prop}$ into the propositional fragment of the JTS forcing
translation as a Heyting algebra homomorphism, with $\triangleright$
as the step-indexed-specific content lying outside the embedded
image.

\paragraph{Limitations and future directions.}
We work at the level of plain Hoare triples with a partial-correctness
wp predicate, without separation logic, heap state, or concurrency.
Whether the forcing reading scales gracefully to Iris-style separation
logic, resource state, invariants, and concurrent step counters is an
open question this paper does not answer; our contribution is the
diagnostic and the negative result, not an Iris-scale framework.
Several natural extensions suggest themselves.  Mechanizing the
type-level half of the JTS correspondence
(Section~\ref{sec:bridge}) as a theorem rather than a heuristic, and
exhibiting non-trivial verifications at richer forcing structures
including transfinite step indices~\cite{spies2021transfinite}, both
remain to be done.  The $\lambda$-calculus development of
Section~\ref{sec:lam} is untyped; lifting it to a simply-typed or
polymorphic source language would test whether the recursion-locus
diagnostic continues to track the higher-order/first-order split when
types constrain the recursion, or whether it instead splits along the
type structure.  The pointwise-product forcing structure
$\mathtt{prod\_FS}$ of Section~\ref{sec:abstract} is defined and
satisfies the axioms, but we do not develop a Hoare logic over it; a
two-counter Hoare logic --- the natural setting for a concurrent
calculus where each component has its own step index --- is the most
direct payoff of having the framework parametric in the forcing
structure, and is the test of whether the parametricity buys
genuinely new verifications.  Lifting the IMP and $\lambda$-calculus
developments of Sections~\ref{sec:imp}--\ref{sec:lam} to be parametric
in the forcing structure is a prerequisite for this and is mostly
bookkeeping.  Whether the recursion-locus diagnostic can be sharpened
to a syntactic check on iProp definitions, applicable automatically
to Iris-style soundness proofs, is the question we are most interested
in pursuing.

The broader point we hope the paper makes is that step-indexed
reasoning, viewed as a forcing construction, sits at the intersection
of three research traditions that do not usually talk to each other:
set-theoretic forcing, modal logic of provability, and step-indexed
semantics for verification.  Each contains piece-of-the-picture
insights that the others could profit from; the mechanization
accompanying this paper is intended as evidence for the technical
claims and as a starting point for others wishing to explore the
intersection further.
