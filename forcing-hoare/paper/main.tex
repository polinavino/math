\begin{abstract}
We give two technical observations about step-indexed Hoare logic,
made mechanically precise by re-presenting the technique as
\emph{ramified forcing} --- Cohen's original forcing construction
stratified by a well-founded hierarchy of levels.

First, we exhibit a structural diagnostic that distinguishes when the
internal L├Čb rule is the natural soundness tool for a recursive
Hoare-style rule.  For the \texttt{while} rule of IMP, internal L├Čb
and meta-level induction on the step index produce structurally
identical proofs; we mechanize both side by side.  For the
\texttt{fix} rule of an untyped $\lambda$-calculus, the soundness
predicate is itself recursive in the program being verified --- a
difference in what we call the \emph{recursion locus} --- and only
the internal L├Čb rule plays to that structure.  This is, to our
knowledge, the first explicit and mechanized articulation of when
the internal-L├Čb idiom of Iris and related systems is essential
versus when it is a presentational choice.

Second, we show that the well-foundedness of the forcing notion is
\emph{essential}: we exhibit a would-be forcing structure over
$\mathbb{Z}$ in which every iProp construction goes through, but
L├Čb's rule fails on $\bot$.  This is a step-indexed-Hoare-logic
counterpart of a classical fact about G├Čdel--L├Čb modal logic; the
statement does not, as far as we can determine, appear in the
verification literature.

The two observations rest on a forcing-style re-presentation of
step-indexed Hoare logic that we develop in Rocq alongside them: a
propositional layer, Hoare logic over IMP and over a $\lambda$-calculus
with \texttt{fix}, an abstract framework parametric in the forcing
notion, and a propositional-fragment embedding of meta-level
$\mathsf{Prop}$ into the Jaber--Tabareau--Sozeau forcing translation
of CIC.  The complete development is approximately 800 lines of Rocq
across 11 files, with no external dependencies.
\end{abstract}
